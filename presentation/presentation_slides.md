## Slide 2: Introduction to Numerical Analysis
### Why Do We Need Iterative Computations?
*   **Linear vs. Nonlinear Engineering**:
    *   Many structural, fluid, and mechanical systems are governed by non-linear physical interactions.
    *   **Analytical Solutions**: Finding exact closed-form algebra is often impossible.
    *   **Numerical Solutions**: Give approximations to mathematical roots using recursive calculations.
*   **Root-Finding**:
    *   Find the exact or near-exact value $x^*$ such that:
        $$f(x^*) = 0$$

## Slide 3: Newton-Raphson Method
### Mathematical Foundation & Derivation
*   Derived from the first-order **Taylor Series Expansion** of $f(x)$ about an estimate $x_n$:
    $$f(x_{n+1}) \approx f(x_n) + f'(x_n)(x_{n+1} - x_n)$$
*   By setting $f(x_{n+1}) = 0$, we solve for the next iterate $x_{n+1}$:
    $$x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$$
*   **Geometric Representation**:
    *   Draws a tangent line at $(x_n, f(x_n))$ and finds its intercept on the x-axis, which defines $x_{n+1}$.

## Slide 4: Convergence Rate & Fail-Safes
### Quadratic Convergence and Solver Robustness
*   **Quadratic Convergence**:
    *   Error at step $n+1$ is proportional to the square of error at step $n$: $e_{n+1} \approx C \cdot e_n^2$.
    *   Correct decimals approximately double at each iteration.
*   **Critical Exceptions Handled by the Solver**:
    1.  **Zero-Derivative Avoidance**: Triggers shutdown if $f'(x) \approx 0$ to prevent division-by-zero.
    2.  **Runaway Divergence**: Stops iterations if iterates head to infinity.
    3.  **Oscillation Cycle Detection**: Detects repeating coordinate walks.

## Slide 5: Application 1 - Beam Deflection Coordinates
### Finding Limit Points on a Supported Beam
*   **Problem Statement**: Calculate the coordinate $x$ on a supported beam where deflection reaches the target allowed limit $\delta_{target}$.
*   **Physical Formulas Evaluated**:
    1.  **Concentrated Central Load $P$** (Cubic Equation):
        $$4 x^3 - 3 L^2 x + \frac{48 E I \delta_{target}}{P} = 0$$
    2.  **Uniformly Distributed Load $w$** (Quartic Equation):
        $$x^4 - 2 L x^3 + L^3 x - \frac{24 E I \delta_{target}}{w} = 0$$
*   **Boundary Validation**: Roots are discarded unless they lie in the beam domain $[0, L]$.

## Slide 6: Application 2 - Nonlinear Elastic Spring
### Displacement of High-Deformation Stiffening Spring
*   **Problem Statement**: Hooke's Law ($F_s = kx$) fails for large deformations.
*   **Physics Formulation**:
    *   Cubic stiffening spring restoring force:
        $$F_s(x) = k_1 x + k_3 x^3$$
    *   Equilibrium equation under load $F_{applied}$:
        $$k_3 x^3 + k_1 x - F_{applied} = 0$$
*   **Key Comparison**: How much does the stiffening term reduce deflection compared to Hooke's linear expectation ($x_{linear} = F/k_1$)?

## Slide 10: Guess Sensitivity Analysis
### Why the Starting Guess $x_0$ Matters
*   Evaluating $f(x) = x^3 - 5x = 0$ ($x^* = 0, \pm 2.236$):
    1.  **Convergent Guess ($x_0 = 2.0$)**: Converges cleanly to $2.236$ in 4 steps.
    2.  **Flat Region Guess ($x_0 \approx 1.291$)**: Derivative $f'(x) \approx 0$. Solver blocks zero division.
    3.  **Oscillating Guess ($x_0 = 1.0$)**: Iterates cycle between $+1.0$ and $-1.0$. Solver flags cycle oscillation.

## Slide 11: Conclusion & Future Scope
*   **Key Takeaways**:
    *   Newton-Raphson is highly efficient (converges under 5 steps) due to its quadratic rate.
    *   Integrating symbolic differentiation removes manual calculations, making the solver robust.
    *   Linear spring and structural beam assumptions must incorporate cubic terms for high loadings.
*   **Future Scope**:
    *   Implement Bisection and Secant methods for convergence comparisons.
    *   Extend to systems of equations using Jacobian matrices.
